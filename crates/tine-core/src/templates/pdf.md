- # PDF annotation
	- Open a PDF that lives in your graph's `assets/` (or drop one in) and read it in a side pane right next to your notes.
	- **Select text** in the PDF to highlight it; click a highlight again to change its color.
	- **Ctrl+drag** a rectangle to clip a figure or an equation as an image highlight.
	- Every highlight becomes a **block** on a notes page named after the PDF, so you can write underneath it, link it, and reference it like any other bullet — and each note jumps straight back to its spot in the document.
	- It's how you turn a paper into linked, searchable notes without ever leaving Tine.
- ## Create one yourself
	- 1. Put a PDF in your graph's `assets/` folder, or drop it into a page.
	- 2. Open the PDF from its asset link so it appears in the side pane.
	- 3. Select text to create a highlight, or hold **Ctrl** and drag a rectangle for an area highlight.
	- 4. Write notes under the generated highlight blocks.
	- 5. What you should see: each highlight is a normal block that links back to the exact PDF location.
